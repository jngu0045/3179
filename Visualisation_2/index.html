A Look into Endangered Species

This visualisation aims to provide insights on the current status and threats to endangered species.

What is the Red List Index

Red List Index measures change in aggregate extinction risk across groups of species. It is based on genuine changes in the number of species in each category of extinction risk on The IUCN Red List of Threatened Species (www.iucnredlist.org) is expressed as changes in an index ranging from 0 to 1. The Red List Index value ranges from 1 (all species are categorized as ‘Least Concern') to 0 (all species are categorized as ‘Extinct'), and so indicates how far the set of species has moved overall towards extinction.

IUCN Categories

The International Union for Conservation of Nature’s (IUCN) Red List is an indicator of biodiversity at a global level. The catergories include “Not Evaluated”, “Data Deficient”, “Least Concern”, “Near Threatened”, “Vulnerable”, “Critically Endangered”, “Extinct in the Wild” and “Extinct”.

It can be seen that plants have the highest number of species classified as endangered. 12350 plant species are classified as endangered, 1050 are classified as vulnerable and 427 as critical.

A Deeper Look into Threats

Invasive weeds are the number one threat to Australian species, followed by agriculture and aquaculture.
